Yelp Redesign: Communicating Authenticity in Reviews

Overview

How often do online reviews reflect the quality of a business or service? Which do you consider more authentic, recommendations from influencers, or from close friends? In Feburary of 2023, we designed a social platform on the Yelp mobile app to improve customer trust in online reviews and increase appeal of the app to a younger user demographic. This involved redesigning the user flow for adding friends and viewing friends’ reviews, and creating a new social activity page on the Yelp app.

This case study will walk through our entire design process, from empathizing with users, defining the problem, ideating solutions, creating low-fidelity prototypes, and conducting the first round of user tests.

Timeline

This was a 5 week project conducted within a remote setting, using Zoom and Slack as major modes of communication.

The Team

The team consisted of five members including myself (the project manager) and four other UX/UI design students.

My Role

My individual role was to organize and facilitate team discussions, and recognize knowledge gaps and plan accordingly for user research, iterating low-fidelity wireframes, and prototyping the final deliverable.

Defining the main objective

My team started by narrowing down the scope of the project to a specific topic: changing the way travelers discover new locations and inspiring people to explore their local area.

💡 How can we create a social media platform that allows travelers to explore destinations they are planning on visiting which shows information and locally recommended spots to visit within a radius?

Jumping on the trends of short-form video content on popular social apps at the time, we proposed to design a new social media platform for content creators to share their local recommendations in the form of short-form videos, and for audiences to discover new spots using the visual information provided with these videos.
